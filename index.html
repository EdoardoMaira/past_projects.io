About
I work with data end to end — from raw records to conclusions people can act on.

Over the past year at Copenhagen Business School I have handled large administrative datasets from Statistics Denmark, built cleaning and validation pipelines in Stata and R, and turned messy data into clear, documented results. Before that, an internship at Intesa Sanpaolo and the OCF certification gave me a first look at how financial data drives real decisions. My projects below span machine learning, econometrics, and behavioural experiments — each built from the data up.

Predicting stock market participation

Analysed the 2022 US Survey of Consumer Finances: cleaned 356 variables across 23,000 households, addressed multicollinearity, and benchmarked six models from logistic regression to SVM, random forest, and gradient boosting.

FINANCIAL ECONOMETRICS · BLOOMBERG

SPX vs Dow Jones: prices & volumes

Modelled 14 years of daily data with ARIMA(2,0,2) and GARCH(1,1) volatility under a t-distribution, then used Johansen and Granger tests to trace the lead-lag and cointegration structure between the two indices.

BEHAVIOURAL FINANCE · QUALTRICS + STATA

Gender priming & financial choices

Designed and ran a randomised survey experiment on how gender-identity priming affects risk-taking. Built composite belief and risky-share indexes from primary data and estimated the treatment effect with OLS.
